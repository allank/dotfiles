# PM Deliverable Standards

## Core Principles

### Deliverable Quality
All outputs should be:
- **Polished**: Ready to share with stakeholders or leadership
- **Structured**: Clear hierarchy, logical flow, easy to scan
- **Actionable**: Include next steps, decisions needed, or recommendations
- **Contextual**: Show relevant data, research, or market context

### Communication Style
- **Executive-ready**: Crisp, scannable, decision-oriented
- **Lead with insights**: Start with "so what?" not just "what"
- **Show your work**: Include rationale for recommendations
- **Visualize when helpful**: Tables, comparison matrices, timelines

---

## Document Types & Formats

### Strategy Documents (PRDs, Roadmaps, Strategy Memos)
- Include executive summary at top
- Clear section headers with logical progression
- Decision framework or recommendation section
- Use standard PRD structure: Problem, Goals, User Stories, Requirements, Success Metrics

### Analysis (Competitive Analysis, Market Research)
- Lead with key findings and implications
- Use comparison tables or matrices
- Include source citations
- Provide "so what?" conclusions

### Stakeholder Communication (Updates, Proposals)
- BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) format
- Bullet points for scannability
- Highlight decisions needed or actions requested
- Include relevant context without overwhelming

### Planning (Roadmaps, Timelines, Prioritization)
- Visual when possible (Gantt charts, kanban-style layouts)
- Show dependencies and sequencing
- Include prioritization rationale (RICE scores, effort/impact matrices)

## Common PM Tasks

### Product Requirements (PRDs)
- Include user personas and use cases
- Specify acceptance criteria clearly
- Add open questions section for alignment

### Competitive Analysis
- Create comparison matrices (features, pricing, positioning)
- Identify gaps and opportunities
- Include market positioning insights
- Note likely roadmap direction based on public info

### User Research Synthesis
- Organize by themes/patterns, not user-by-user
- Quantify when possible ("8 of 12 users mentioned...")
- Link insights to product implications
- Include representative quotes

### Roadmap Planning
- Show themes/bets, not just features
- Include rough sizing (T-shirt sizes or story points)
- Note dependencies between items
- Align to business goals or OKRs

### Business Cases
- Frame the opportunity (market size, user pain)
- Estimate impact (revenue, engagement, efficiency)
- Outline costs and resources needed
- Provide recommendation with confidence level

### Stakeholder Updates
- Start with status (Green/Yellow/Red)
- Highlight key decisions or blockers
- Show progress against goals
- Keep it brief and action-oriented

## Research Best Practices
- **Multi-source validation**: Cross-reference claims across sources
- **Recency matters**: Prioritize current data, note when information may be outdated
- **Synthesize, don't summarize**: Provide insights and patterns, not just facts
- **Quantify when possible**: Include metrics, benchmarks, market sizing
- **Flag assumptions**: Be explicit about what's known vs. estimated

---

## Quality Checks

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ Analysis without clear business implications
- ❌ Walls of text with no structure or hierarchy
- ❌ Recommendations without supporting rationale
- ❌ Outdated market data presented as current
- ❌ Vague success metrics or goals
- ❌ Documents that aren't ready to share with stakeholders

### Green Flags to Embrace
- ✅ Executive summaries that enable quick decisions
- ✅ Data-backed insights with clear "so what?"
- ✅ Structured frameworks that bring clarity
- ✅ Proactive identification of risks or blockers
- ✅ Cross-functional perspective (eng, design, marketing, sales)
- ✅ Actionable next steps with clear ownership
