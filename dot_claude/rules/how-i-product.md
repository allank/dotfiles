# How Allan Does Product

## Personality & Working Style
- Enneagram Type 5 / ENTP
- Enjoys complex problems and systems thinking
- Prone to side quests and deep dives ([relevant xkcd](https://xkcd.com/356/))
- Programmer mindset: automate friction, build tools to scratch itches
- Can be impatient; prefers doing over delegating
- Core texts: *Thinking in Systems* (Meadows), *Intuition Pumps* (Dennett)

## Product Philosophy
- The PM's purpose is to ensure the team works on **impactful and meaningful work**
- This spans: strategic planning → sense-making → contextualising and preparing work
- Bias toward understanding the *why* before the *what*
- **Outcomes over outputs**: A shipped feature is not success—changed behaviour or measurable business results are. Ask "what will be different?" not "what will we build?"


## Thinking Frameworks
For strategic thinking and problem-solving, use the **thinking-partner** skill, which covers:
- Three Horizons (H1/H2/H3 mapping)
- Idealized Design (constraint-free system design)
- Dennett's Intuition Pumps (Jootsing, Occam's Broom, Knobs & Sliders, etc.)

Additional frameworks I apply:
- **Cynefin**: Categorise problem type → match response to domain
- **Wardley Mapping**: Strategic positioning and build vs buy decisions

## Working With Allan
- Challenge ideas rigorously—I value pushback over agreement
- Steelman my position before critiquing it (Rapoport's Rules)
- When I'm in "side quest" mode, help me assess whether it's worth the detour
- Surface systems-level implications; don't just solve the immediate problem
- When reviewing my work, apply the same frameworks I use
- For expansive thinking sessions, invoke the `thinking-partner` skill
